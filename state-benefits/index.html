Social Security Bureau

State Benefits & Social Guarantees

Under Article 40-45 of the USSR Constitution, all Soviet citizens possess guaranteed rights to communal housing, universal health protection, old-age pensions, free education, and state allocation vouchers.

Housing Quotas

Communal Housing & Apartment Allocations

Ministry of Municipal Economy & Civil Welfare

Housing in the Soviet Union is guaranteed by the state without mortgage debt. Registered workers receive subsidized apartments or room shares based on family size, seniority credits, and regional propiska residency registrations.

Standard Apartment Voucher

Guaranteed

Assigned to multi-person families according to sanitary norms (9m² per person). Minimal rent (under 3% of monthly income).

Priority Worker Queue

Stakhanovite Rank

Industrial innovators, union delegates, and heroic workers skip standard queue waiting periods for newly built block apartments.

Gosplan Supplies

Food Provisions & Staple Ration Coupons

State Committee for Supplies & Domestic Commerce

Essential food staples—including bread, dairy, meat, and seasonal produce—are strictly price-controlled by the state to shield citizens from market speculation. Specialized coupon registers ensure equitable distribution across all Soviet republics.

Fixed Price Guarantee

Basic wheat bread and milk prices remain pegged to official Gosplan indices regardless of global inflation.

Trade Union Store Allocations

Factory workers receive seasonal fruit crates, tea allowances, and holiday hamper passes directly through local Soviet syndicates.

Pension Security

Retirement Pensions & Invalidity Protection

State Board of Social Maintenance

Retirement age in the USSR is among the lowest globally (age 60 for men, 55 for women, with earlier retirement for heavy industrial laborers). Pensions are calculated directly from highest-earning labor years without required private savings.

Citizen FAQ

How do I register for a propiska?

Propiska residence registrations are issued by local Soviet militia offices on Discord after citizen verification.

Are healthcare services completely free?

Yes. Under Soviet constitutional law, medical treatment and emergency care require zero out-of-pocket payment.
